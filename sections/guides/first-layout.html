Building Your First Layout Guide

Every web page needs structure — where does the sidebar go? How wide is the content area? How does it look on a phone? This guide teaches you Vanduo's grid system from the ground up, with live examples you can tweak and learn from.

The Mental Model: Container → Row → Columns

Vanduo's grid has three layers. Think of it like a bookshelf:

  • Container — The bookshelf itself. Centers your content and sets the maximum width.
  • Row — A shelf. Creates a horizontal strip that holds columns.
  • Columns — The books on the shelf. Each row splits into 12 equal columns, and you decide how many each piece of content gets.
<div class="vd-container">           <!-- The bookshelf -->
    <div class="vd-row">              <!-- A shelf -->
        <div class="vd-col-6">...</div> <!-- Left half (6 of 12) -->
        <div class="vd-col-6">...</div> <!-- Right half (6 of 12) -->
    </div>
</div>
Why 12 columns? Because 12 divides evenly by 2, 3, 4, and 6 — giving you maximum flexibility. Want 2 equal columns? 6+6. Three columns? 4+4+4. Four columns? 3+3+3+3. A sidebar + main? 3+9.

Step 1 of 5 The Container

Every layout starts with a container. You have two choices:

vd-container

Fixed max-width, centered. Gets wider at each breakpoint. Best for most pages.

<div class="vd-container">
    <!-- Content is centered with
         max-width at each breakpoint -->
</div>

vd-container-fluid

Full width, always. Stretches to the edge of the viewport. Best for dashboards or wide layouts.

<div class="vd-container-fluid">
    <!-- Content stretches to fill
         the full viewport width -->
</div>
Pro tip: You can also use responsive containers like vd-container-md or vd-container-lg that become fluid below their breakpoint. This gives you full-width on mobile and centered on desktop.

Step 3 of 5 Responsive Breakpoints

The real power of the grid is making layouts adapt to different screen sizes. Add a breakpoint suffix to your column classes:

Prefix Breakpoint Typical Device
vd-col-0px+ (always)All screens
vd-col-sm-576px+Phones (landscape)
vd-col-md-768px+Tablets
vd-col-lg-992px+Laptops
vd-col-xl-1200px+Desktops
vd-col-2xl-1400px+Large desktops

Example: Full width on mobile, 2 columns on tablet, 3 columns on desktop:

Item 1
Item 2
Item 3
<div class="vd-row">
    <div class="vd-col-12 vd-col-md-6 vd-col-lg-4">Item 1</div>
    <div class="vd-col-12 vd-col-md-6 vd-col-lg-4">Item 2</div>
    <div class="vd-col-12 vd-col-md-6 vd-col-lg-4">Item 3</div>
</div>
<!--
    Mobile (0-767px):     1 column  (vd-col-12 = full width each)
    Tablet (768-991px):   2 columns (vd-col-md-6 = half width each)
    Desktop (992px+):     3 columns (vd-col-lg-4 = one-third each)
-->
Pro tip: Always design mobile-first. Start with vd-col-12 (full width), then add breakpoint overrides like vd-col-md-6 and vd-col-lg-4 for wider screens.

Step 5 of 5 Offsets and Ordering

Offsets push columns to the right by a number of column widths. Great for centering a narrow column:

vd-col-6 vd-offset-3 (centered)
<div class="vd-row">
    <!-- 3 columns of empty space + 6 columns of content + 3 remaining = centered -->
    <div class="vd-col-6 vd-offset-3">Centered content</div>
</div>

Ordering lets you rearrange columns without changing your HTML. Useful when you want a sidebar on the right on desktop but on top on mobile:

<div class="vd-row">
    <!-- On mobile: sidebar appears first (order-1), main second (order-2)
         On desktop: main appears first (md-order-1), sidebar second (md-order-2) -->
    <div class="vd-col-12 vd-col-md-3 vd-order-1 vd-order-md-2">Sidebar</div>
    <div class="vd-col-12 vd-col-md-9 vd-order-2 vd-order-md-1">Main Content</div>
</div>

Common Layout Recipes

Copy-paste these patterns to quickly build common page layouts:

Blog Layout

<div class="vd-container">
    <div class="vd-row">
        <main class="vd-col-12 vd-col-lg-8">
            <!-- Blog posts go here -->
        </main>
        <aside class="vd-col-12 vd-col-lg-4">
            <!-- Sidebar: recent posts, tags, about -->
        </aside>
    </div>
</div>

Dashboard Layout

<div class="vd-container-fluid">
    <div class="vd-row">
        <nav class="vd-col-12 vd-col-lg-2">
            <!-- Sidebar navigation -->
        </nav>

            <div class="vd-row">
                <div class="vd-col-12 vd-col-md-6 vd-col-xl-3">Stat Card 1</div>

                <div class="vd-col-12 vd-col-md-6 vd-col-xl-3">Stat Card 4</div>
            </div>
        </main>
    </div>
</div>

Landing Page

<div class="vd-container">

Pro tip: Open your browser's DevTools (F12) and toggle the responsive design mode to see your layout adapt in real time. This is the best way to learn how breakpoints feel.
